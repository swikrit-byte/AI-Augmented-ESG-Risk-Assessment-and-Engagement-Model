Source: Environmental Compliance Report 2023 · TNFD LEAP Framework · ESRS E4
▼ Occupational Health & Safety S Critical 82 LTIFR 2.8 vs sector 2.1; 1 fatality FY2022 Short €25M
Root Cause
LTIFR 2.8 is 33% above sector median 2.1. One fatality in FY2022 (forklift incident, Taranto). H&S management system ISO 45001 certified at 3 of 5 sites. Contractor H&S oversight rated weak — 38% of contractors without documented pre-qualification.
Financial Exposure
Fatality litigation: €8–15M per incident. Regulatory penalties: €500k–2M per ILO/EU OSH breach. Production stoppage: est. €12M/week. Reputational impact on recruitment in tight labour market.
Mitigation Required
1. Extend ISO 45001 to all 5 sites. 2. Mandatory contractor H&S pre-qualification. 3. LTIFR target below sector median by FY2025. 4. Executive H&S KPI in remuneration.
Source: Sustainability Report 2023, pp.44–51 · ISO 45001 Audit Summary · ESRS S1 · ILO Convention 155
▼ Human Rights & Supply Chain Due Diligence S Critical 76 No HRDD policy; no supplier code; Brazil/DRC sourcing Short–Medium €30M+
Root Cause
No Human Rights Due Diligence (HRDD) Policy. No Supplier Code of Conduct. Iron ore sourced from Brazil (Vale — tailings dam risk) and DRC (conflict minerals exposure). EU Corporate Sustainability Due Diligence Directive (CSDDD) in scope from 2027.
Financial Exposure
CSDDD non-compliance: fines up to 5% of global turnover (€60M). Supply chain disruption from supplier incident: est. €30M+ production impact. Reputational exposure from NGO/media investigation.
Mitigation Required
1. Adopt UN Guiding Principles-aligned HRDD Policy. 2. Deploy Supplier Code of Conduct + SAQ. 3. Prioritise tier-1 supplier audits in Brazil/DRC. 4. Establish grievance mechanism. Timeline: 12 months.
Source: Sustainability Report 2023 (gap) · UNGP 17–21 · OECD Due Diligence Guidance · ESRS S2 · EU CSDDD
▼ Workforce Diversity & Social Inclusion S High 62 22% female workforce; no pay equity audit; no DEI targets Medium €6M
Female workforce at 22% (EU manufacturing average: 28%). No gender pay equity audit disclosed. No quantitative DEI targets. EU Pay Transparency Directive (2023/970) requires pay equity reporting from 2026 for companies with 100+ employees. Risk of investor engagement on gender equality and potential exclusion from gender-lens investment screens.
Source: Sustainability Report 2023, pp.52–56 · EU Pay Transparency Directive · ESRS S1
▼ Community Relations & Social Licence S High 58 Taranto community health litigation; no grievance mechanism Short €12M
Ongoing community health litigation in Taranto related to air quality and industrial emissions (est. €8–12M contingent liability). No formal grievance mechanism or community engagement protocol. ESRS S3 (Affected Communities) requires disclosure of community engagement and remedy processes. Social licence to operate at Taranto contingent on IED permit renewal and emission reductions.
Source: Annual Report 2023 (legal section) · ESRS S3 · UN Guiding Principles Pillar III
▼ Labour Relations & Workforce Transition S Medium 44 Strong union coverage; low turnover 9.2%; reskilling gap Medium–Long €9M
Strong union coverage (87%) provides labour stability but constrains operational flexibility. Turnover of 9.2% is below sector average — positive retention signal. However, just transition risk is material: DRI/EAF adoption would displace ~800–1,200 BF-BOF roles. No workforce transition plan or reskilling programme disclosed. Training hours (24 hrs/yr) below best practice (40+ hrs).
Source: HR Report 2023 · ESRS S1 · ILO Just Transition Guidelines
▼ Ownership Concentration & Governance G High 68 Fondazione 41%; related-party transactions undisclosed Ongoing €20M
Fondazione Verda holds 41% — de facto control without formal control premium. Fondazione board members overlap with company board (3 dual appointments). Related-party transactions with Fondazione entities not separately disclosed in Annual Report. Private equity stake (28%) creates exit pressure that could conflict with long-term ESG investment horizon. ESRS G1 requires transparent related-party disclosure.
Source: Annual Report 2023, pp.88–96 · Corporate Governance Report · ESRS G1
▼ Whistleblower & Speak-Up Culture G High 65 No formal mechanism; EU Directive 2019/1937 non-compliant Short €5M
EU Whistleblower Protection Directive (2019/1937) required implementation by December 2021 for companies with 250+ employees. Where the company has no formal, anonymous digital reporting channel, Regulatory fine risk: up to €50k under Italian implementation (D.Lgs. 24/2023). Reputational risk if undisclosed misconduct surfaces. Governance rating agencies (ISS, Glass Lewis) flag this as material gap.
Source: Governance gap (not in any disclosure) · EU Directive 2019/1937 · D.Lgs. 24/2023 Italy · ESRS G1
▼ ESG Remuneration Linkage G High 58 No ESG KPIs in executive LTIP; investor pressure increasing Short–Medium €3M
Executive remuneration tied solely to financial targets (EBITDA, ROCE). No ESG KPIs in short- or long-term incentive plans. 78% of MSCI-rated NACE C24 peers now include climate/H&S metrics in executive pay. Institutional investors (Norges Bank, CalPERS) have filed say-on-pay objections at comparable companies without ESG linkage. Suggested LTIP KPIs: Scope 1 trajectory, LTIFR, CSRD readiness.
Source: Remuneration Report 2023 · ESRS G1 · ISS Proxy Advisory Guidelines
▼ Anti-Corruption & Business Ethics G Medium 42 Code of Conduct in place; training 24 hrs; no incidents reported Ongoing €4M
Code of Conduct adopted and published. Anti-bribery and anti-corruption (ABAC) policy in place covering all employees. Zero incidents or enforcement actions reported FY2021–2023. ISO 37001 certification not pursued. Third-party due diligence for agents in non-EU markets is basic — no structured risk-based screening tool. Operations in Spain face minor Transparency International CPI risk (score 62/100).
Source: Code of Conduct 2023 · Annual Report 2023, pp.101–104 · ESRS G1 · ISO 37001
▼ CSRD / ESRS Reporting Readiness G High 61 FY2025 obligation; DMA incomplete; ESRS gaps material Short €8M
A large public-interest entity (PIE) has a CSRD obligation from the FY2025 reporting cycle. Double Materiality Assessment (DMA) in early stages — not investor-grade. Key ESRS gaps: E1 (no transition plan), S2 (no HRDD), G1 (no whistleblower policy, no RPT disclosure). Estimated CSRD readiness programme cost: €3–8M over 2 years (data infrastructure, assurance, reporting systems). Regulatory compliance risk if FY2025 report is materially deficient.
Source: Gap analysis conducted during this assessment · ESRS 1/2 cross-cutting standards · CSRD Directive 2022/2464/EU
